Impact of Marketing on society

Once you start your career path in the world of Business and Entrepreneurship, you come across the term “marketing” quite often. There is a common misconception that marketing involves nothing more than selling things or that it is merely the realm of business corporations. A straightforward definition of marketing is difficult to give since the term covers a wide range of activities and variables. Now, what is Marketing?

“Marketing refers to any actions a company takes to attract an audience to the company's product or services through high-quality messaging. It is a total system of interacting business activities designed to plan, price, promote and distribute want-satisfying products and services to provide and potential customers.” Now you may be asking yourself, what does marketing do? What is the purpose of marketing? Marketing is the process of getting people interested in your company's product or service. Research, analysis, and an understanding of your ideal customer's interests are necessary to accomplish this.

We are surrounded by marketing methods every day, so we are constantly exposed to them. Marketing helps society achieve higher and better living standards. How? As previously said, marketing is a strategy employed by businesses to better understand their customers and produce better products. As a result, marketing aims to raise the level of living by developing relevant programs for various groups of society. If a buyer is interested in a product and purchases it, there is a chance that it may improve their level of living. It could be something luxurious or provide them with any level of comfort. The goal of marketing is not to raise the standard of living, but the side effects may accomplish so. This is most likely one of the most significant societal effects of marketing.



Informing Consumers: It plays a huge role in informing and educating consumers. When a company launches its new product, marketing helps consumers understand what the product is and how it may help them. Companies generally begin the marketing by realizing customers' needs and explaining how their products can assist in meeting those needs. Marketers provide information about new products and what they can do for customers with specific needs. It’s all about learning and growing.

Managing Consumer Expectations: Aside from informing customers about the product, marketing also helps set and manage customer expectations. Brand loyalty and the trust of the brand are what consumers look for. The marketing function of businesses involves informing consumers of major changes affecting product offerings or seeking to improve product quality. This allows consumers to make an informed decision. There are positive and negative effects of managing consumer expectations.

In a nutshell, marketing is like an enormous umbrella that encompasses all the efforts that companies, brands, and individuals use to achieve their goals. It is a global language. Marketing has affected our daily lives in many ways and is a vital part of the world of entrepreneurship and business.
